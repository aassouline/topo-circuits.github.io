European ERC Project: ANYONBOX

Motivation

Protecting quantum information from environmental interference is essential for advancing quantum technologies. However, reading a quantum state or performing gate operations inevitably involves coupling to the environment, leading to loss of coherence.

To overcome this limitation, topological phases of matter provide a promising route for robust quantum information storage and manipulation through non-local degrees of freedom.

Topological Quantum Matter

A key example is the even-denominator fractional quantum Hall effect in two-dimensional electron systems under strong magnetic fields. In these systems, quasiparticles known as non-abelian anyons emerge and share a non-local degree of freedom called the topological charge.

However, detecting and manipulating this topological charge remains experimentally challenging in conventional semiconductor platforms due to narrow stability ranges and limited tunability.

The ANYONBOX Concept

The ANYONBOX project exploits the unique properties of bilayer graphene, which hosts robust candidate states for non-abelian anyons. Using van der Waals heterostructures, we engineer electrically tunable potential wells that isolate individual anyons: the so-called anyon boxes.

The addition of a single anyon into the box is probed via a combination of quantum capacitance and thermal noise measurements, allowing access to its thermodynamic response.

Objectives

  • Detect the topological charge of individual anyons in bilayer graphene
  • Measure coherence properties of isolated anyons
  • Explore controlled manipulation via non-abelian braiding operations

Outlook

Realizing anyon boxes in bilayer graphene will provide a new experimental platform to probe topological quantum states, with the long-term goal of enabling fault-tolerant quantum information processing.
