Electron Transport via Surface Acoustic Waves

Motivation

Operating electronic devices using single-electron transfer is a key goal of ultra–low-power electronics. It also enables new paradigms such as flying qubits, where quantum information is encoded in the trajectory of a single electron propagating through a circuit.

Physical Principle

Pioneering experiments have demonstrated that individual electrons can be transported using surface acoustic waves. In this regime, electrons are trapped in moving potential minima generated by GHz-frequency acoustic excitations.

Their trajectories and coherence properties can be probed using sensitive quantum detectors such as single-electron transistors and quantum point contacts.

Project Goal

Building on these advances, we aim to develop an electron quantum optics platform based on graphene devices, with the goal of overcoming current limitations in quantum coherence and enabling controlled single-electron manipulation.

Outlook

This platform will provide a basis for studying quantum-coherent single electron transport and may open routes toward on-the-fly manipulation in quantum electronic architectures.
