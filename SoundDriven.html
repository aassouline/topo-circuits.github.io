Project

Operating electronic devices through single-electron transfer is a key goal of ultra–low-power electronics. It also opens the way to new paradigms, such as flying qubits, where quantum information is encoded in the trajectory of a single electron propagating through a circuit. Thanks to pioneering work, it is now possible to transport individual electrons using surface acoustic waves. Their trajectories can be probed using quantum detectors such as single-electron transistors and quantum point contacts. Building on these advances, we aim to develop an electron quantum optics platform based on ultra-clean graphene devices, with the goal of overcoming the limitations in quantum coherence encountered in existing systems.
