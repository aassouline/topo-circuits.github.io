Low-temperature measurement platforms

Our experiments rely on state-of-the-art cryogenic infrastructure enabling electronic transport measurements at ultra-low temperatures and high magnetic fields.

Dilution refrigerator equipped with a 16 T magnet and base temperature of 10 mK.

A cryogen-free ProteoxMX dilution refrigerator from Oxford is expected in June 2026. It will feature a base temperature below 10 mK, a 14 T magnet, a bottom loading mechanism, and a large 360 mm mixing chamber plate, enabling increased experimental flexibility and throughput.

Sample Characterization

We also rely extensively on sample characterization. Our facilities include two atomic force microscopes (DIV and Icon from Bruker) and two scanning electron microscopes equipped with focused ion beam (FIB) systems.
