Alexandre Assouline

Principal Investigator

Alexandre Assouline’s research focuses on the electronic properties of solids in which macroscopic quantum phases emerge from the interaction of a large number of particles. These materials, strictly two-dimensional like graphene, exhibit rich and novel physics due to their reduced dimensionality. In particular, they can host exotic quasiparticles such as anyons, which are of great interest for quantum information because of their topological properties, making them less sensitive to decoherence—one of the major challenges in modern physics. After completing his PhD at ESPCI Paris on the anomalous Josephson effect under the supervision of Hervé Aubin, he joined Preden Roulleau’s group at CEA Saclay, and then Andrea Young’s group at the University of California Santa Barbara as a postdoctoral researcher. Recruited by the CNRS in 2023, he was awarded an ERC grant in 2026, dedicated to the study of anyons for topological quantum information.
