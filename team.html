Team Members

Alexandre Assouline — Principal Investigator

alexandre.assouline@neel.cnrs.fr

Alexandre’s research focuses on quantum materials where macroscopic phases emerge from many-body interactions. In two-dimensional systems such as graphene, reduced dimensionality gives rise to rich physics and exotic quasiparticles such as anyons, of strong interest for topological quantum information.

Mohammad Mulla-Yusuf — PhD Student

mohammad.mulla-yusuf@neel.cnrs.fr

Project: Sound-Driven Quasiparticles

Mohammad studies the transport of single quantum-coherent electrons using surface acoustic waves in ultra-clean graphene devices, enabling controlled electron propagation at GHz frequencies.

Fatima Bachir — Undergraduate Student

fatima.bachir@neel.cnrs.fr

Project: Anyon Box in Bilayer Graphene

Fatima develops electrostatic traps for anyons in fractional quantum Hall systems, probing their fractional charge via quantum capacitance measurements under strong magnetic fields.

Nathan Wack — Undergraduate Student

nathan.wack@neel.cnrs.fr

Project: Anyon Box in Bilayer Graphene

Nathan investigates the entropy of confined anyons by combining quantum capacitance and noise measurements, exploring their internal degrees of freedom at ultra-low temperatures.
